# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They ensure the team follows the agreed process and continuously improves their ways of working.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Remove blockers and impediments for the team
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external distractions
- Track and visualize team metrics (velocity, burndown)
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Enable self-organization and cross-functional collaboration
- Reduce cycle time and improve predictability
- Create a safe environment for experimentation and learning

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups
- Sprint planning and retrospective facilitation
- One-on-ones with team members on process improvements
- Coordination with other Scrum Masters and Project Managers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Collaborate on timeline coordination, risk management, and cross-team dependencies
- **Product Managers**: Ensure backlog is refined and ready for sprint planning
- **Developers**: Remove impediments and support continuous improvement
- **QA/Testing**: Facilitate quality discussions and definition of done

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, design intuitive interfaces, and validate solutions through user testing. They ensure products are usable, accessible, and deliver a positive user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Develop and maintain design systems and style guides
- Collaborate with developers on implementation
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design practices

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Validate solutions with real users before implementation

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and personas
- Prototype walkthroughs with stakeholders
- Collaboration with developers during implementation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on feature definition and user needs
- **Developers**: Partner on implementation and design feasibility
- **Data Analysts**: Use analytics to inform design decisions
- **Support Engineers**: Gather user feedback and pain points

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts collect, analyze, and interpret data to inform product decisions and measure outcomes. They provide insights that guide strategy, feature prioritization, and performance optimization.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Analyze user behavior and product usage patterns
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Support A/B testing and experimentation
- Identify trends and opportunities from data
- Ensure data quality and integrity

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making
- Measure and communicate product impact
- Identify opportunities for optimization
- Reduce uncertainty through evidence

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews with product and engineering
- Ad-hoc analysis requests from stakeholders
- Experiment results and recommendations
- Dashboard updates and insights reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Define success metrics and analyze feature impact
- **UX Designers**: Provide usage analytics to inform design decisions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on instrumentation and data collection
- **Project Managers**: Track project metrics and report on outcomes

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads ensure products and systems are secure by design, identify vulnerabilities, and establish security best practices. They balance security requirements with business and user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Review designs and code for security vulnerabilities
- Define and enforce security policies and standards
- Conduct threat modeling and risk assessments
- Respond to security incidents and vulnerabilities
- Provide security training and guidance to teams
- Ensure compliance with security regulations

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities before production
- Minimize risk of data breaches and security incidents
- Build security awareness across the organization
- Balance security with usability and velocity

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews during design and code review
- Threat model sessions with architecture and engineering
- Incident response coordination
- Security advisories and best practice guidance

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Review code and provide security guidance
- **Project Managers**: Identify security risks and mitigation plans
- **Product Managers**: Balance security requirements with features
- **Support Engineers**: Respond to security-related customer concerns

---

## Support Engineer

### Role Summary
Support Engineers help customers resolve technical issues, gather feedback, and identify product improvements. They serve as the voice of the customer and ensure smooth adoption and usage.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to customer support tickets and escalations
- Troubleshoot technical issues and provide solutions
- Document known issues and workarounds
- Gather and relay customer feedback to product teams
- Create and maintain support documentation
- Identify patterns in support requests for product improvements

### Goals
- Resolve customer issues quickly and effectively
- Improve customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce repeat support requests through documentation
- Surface product improvements from customer feedback

### Typical Communication
- Daily customer interactions via tickets and chat
- Weekly summaries of common issues and trends
- Escalation coordination with engineering
- Knowledge base articles and FAQs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share customer feedback and feature requests
- **Developers**: Escalate bugs and collaborate on issue resolution
- **UX Designers**: Provide insights on user pain points
- **Data Analysts**: Contribute to understanding usage patterns
